# AI Documentary Studio - SRS Volume 02.5 Part 05 - Motion Timeline Engine

**Version:** 1.0 **Status:** Critical **Priority:** Highest

## Objective
Convert static images into cinematic visual sequences. Every movement must have narrative purpose. Motion exists to guide attention.

## Position
Synchronization Engine → Motion Timeline Engine → Rendering Engine

## Camera Path Planner
Instead of zoom→stop: Hold → Slow Push → Tiny Drift → Hold → Soft Pan. Motion feels like one continuous camera.

## Ken Burns Engine
Push In, Push Out, Diagonal Push, Corner Push, Vertical Pan, Horizontal Pan, Mixed Motion. Must respect composition.

## Parallax Planner
Separate foreground/middle/background. Each layer moves independently. Motion must remain subtle.

## Camera Target
Every movement has a destination: character face, main object, highlighted word, symbol, environment. Never move without a target.

## Attention Engine
Priority: Face > Important Object > Highlighted Word > Supporting Objects > Background

## Easing System
Linear, Ease In, Ease Out, Ease In-Out, Cinematic Ease. Documentary Default. Avoid robotic movement.

## Motion Overlap
Motion may continue across scenes (push → crossfade → continue push → next scene). Creates seamless continuity.

## Camera Breathing
Move → Pause → Move → Hold → Move. Stillness is part of motion.

## Focus Shift
Instead of replacing images: crop, zoom, pan, blur background, light emphasis.

## Word Focus
When word insert appears: camera stops, focuses, returns. Motion never competes with important words.

## Motion Safety
Prevent: face cropping, object cropping, subtitle collision, rapid direction changes, motion sickness.

## Motion Density
Max motion budget per minute. Low density = mostly holds. Medium = push+pan. High = energetic channels only.

## Motion Score
Each movement: Purpose Score, Emotion Score, Smoothness Score, Viewer Comfort Score, Narrative Score.

## Zenn-style Profile
Long slow push-ins, gentle pans, minimal parallax, strong holds, soft easing, camera moves only when narration develops, motion pauses during important words.

## Camera Director AI
Decide where camera should look, plan complete path, predict viewer eye movement, maintain cinematic framing, avoid repetitive patterns.

## Cognitive Camera Engine
Move toward most meaningful object. Priority: Narrative Importance + Visual Importance + Voice Emphasis + Emotion = Camera Target Score.

## Multi-Stage Camera Path
One image: 0-2s Hold → 2-5s Slow Push → 5-7s Tiny Pan → 7-9s Hold → 9-11s Slow Zoom. Prevents static images from feeling lifeless.
